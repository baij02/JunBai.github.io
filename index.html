As a highly motivated and ambitious individual, I am excited to embark on the next chapter of my professional journey as I anticipate my graduation in the Spring of 2023. With a broad range of interests spanning the cutting-edge fields of machine learning, data mining, computer vision, bioinformatics, and medical image analysis, I am eager to contribute my skills and expertise to a dynamic and innovative organization. I have honed my abilities and gained valuable experience through a combination of coursework and hands-on projects, positioning me as a well-rounded and adaptable candidate, ready to hit the ground running in a diverse array of roles. I am confident in my ability to make a meaningful impact in any position within these fields and am eagerly looking forward to exploring the various opportunities available to me.

As a distinguished Ph.D. candidate from the esteemed University of Connecticut Computer Science and Engineering Department, I have had the privilege of working under the guidance of renowned researcher, Dr. Sheida Nabavi, in her esteemed lab. Over the past few years, I have devoted myself to the development of cutting-edge deep learning and traditional computer vision methods to analyze a diverse range of medical images, including X-ray images, Computed Tomosynthesis (CT), histological images, and cell images. My research encompasses a broad spectrum of tasks, including classification, segmentation, synthesizing, and reconstructing images, and has led to significant advancements in the fields of cancer diagnosis, disease prediction, and object reconstruction. My contributions to these fields have been substantial, and I am honored to have played a part in advancing the state of the art in medical image analysis. I am eager to continue my research and make more impactful contributions to this field in the future.

I love research and teaching. They are both important in the field of academia. Research allows for the discovery and advancement of new knowledge and understanding in a particular field. Teaching helps to disseminate this knowledge to the next generation of scholars and practitioners. Together, research and teaching form a symbiotic relationship, with research informing teaching and teaching providing a means to apply and expand upon the knowledge gained through research.

Teaching


Lab Teaching
  • CSE 1010 Introduction to Computing for Engineers (Spring 2020, Fall 2021)
    This course serves as an introduction to the fundamental concepts of computing logic, algorithmic thinking, computing processes, programming languages, and computing environments.
  • CSE 1729 Introduction to Principles of Programming (Spring 2021)
    This course is an introduction to computer programming using a structured programming language. The course will cover the fundamental elements of program design and analysis, and students will learn how to use data and functional abstraction to create correct, efficient, and easy-to-understand programs for a variety of common computing problems.
Teaching Assistant
  • CSE 4102 Programming Languages (Fall 2020)
    This course is focused on the study of programming languages, specifically examining their features and programming paradigms. Topics covered in the course include data types, control structures, run-time environments, and semantics.
  • CSE 3300 Computer Networks and Data Communication (Fall 2019, Spring 2022)
    This course serves as an introduction to the field of computer networks and data communications. The course will cover the fundamental concepts of network types, components, and topology, protocol architecture, routing algorithms, and network performance.
  • CSE 2102 Introduction to Software Engineering (Spring 2021)
    This course covers the fundamental concepts of software engineering including the software life cycle and various software development process models. It will introduce students to the different techniques used in software specification, design methodologies, performance analysis, and verification.
  • CPS 542 Database Management Systems, CPS 562 Advance Database Management Systems (Fall 2018, Spring 2019)
    This course serves as an introduction to database systems, with a focus on modeling and programming databases. Students will learn about E/R modeling, the theoretical concepts of the relational model, and will gain practical experience working with a commercial DBMS.

Software Artical
  • Brain nucleus microscope image analysis user interface tool
    The software is designed to perform multiple functions in relation to 3D object of nucleus microscope images. It can display the images, add annotations to them, perform analysis on the images, register them and reconstruct 3D objects from the images. This software is a powerful tool for researchers and scientists who work with microscope images of nuclei, as it allows them to easily view, analyze, and manipulate the images in a variety of ways. The ability to register and reconstruct 3D objects from the images can also provide valuable insights and information that can aid in scientific research and discovery.
  • Prediction of cancer probability using prior and current year images: A user interface
    The software is developed to assess the capability of a novel deep learning model in predicting clinical trials and compare its performance with radiologists. It will provide a user interface to evaluate the prediction ability of the deep learning model and compare it with the results of radiologists. This software could be useful for researchers and medical practitioners who are working in the field of medical imaging and want to evaluate the accuracy and reliability of deep learning models in predicting clinical outcomes.
