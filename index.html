I am on the market with expected graduation in the Spring 2023. Im broadly interested in machine learning, data mining, computer vision, bioinformatics, and medical image analysis positions.

I am currently a Ph.d. candidate from University of Connecticut Computer Sicence and Engineering Department. I work in Dr. Sheida Nabavi's lab. In the past few years, I spend my time to develop deep learning methods and traditional computer vision methods to analysis medical images includes X-ray images, Computed Tomosynthesis (CT), histological image and cell images. The tasks of my research broad to classification, segmentation, synthesising, and reconstructing images. My research contributed to cancer diagnostic, diease prediction, and reconstructing objects.

I love research and teaching. They are both important in the field of academia. Research allows for the discovery and advancement of new knowledge and understanding in a particular field. Teaching helps to disseminate this knowledge to the next generation of scholars and practitioners. Together, research and teaching form a symbiotic relationship, with research informing teaching and teaching providing a means to apply and expand upon the knowledge gained through research.

Software Artical
  • Brain nucleus microscope image analysis user interface toolThe software is designed to perform multiple functions in relation to 3D object of nucleus microscope images. It can display the images, add annotations to them, perform analysis on the images, register them and reconstruct 3D objects from the images. This software is a powerful tool for researchers and scientists who work with microscope images of nuclei, as it allows them to easily view, analyze, and manipulate the images in a variety of ways. The ability to register and reconstruct 3D objects from the images can also provide valuable insights and information that can aid in scientific research and discovery.
  • Prediction of cancer probability using prior and current year images: A user interfaceThe software is developed to assess the capability of a novel deep learning model in predicting clinical trials and compare its performance with radiologists. It will provide a user interface to evaluate the prediction ability of the deep learning model and compare it with the results of radiologists. This software could be useful for researchers and medical practitioners who are working in the field of medical imaging and want to evaluate the accuracy and reliability of deep learning models in predicting clinical outcomes.
