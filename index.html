I love research and teaching. They are both important in the field of academia. Research allows for the discovery and advancement of new knowledge and understanding in a particular field. Teaching helps to disseminate this knowledge to the next generation of scholars and practitioners. Together, research and teaching form a symbiotic relationship, with research informing teaching and teaching providing a means to apply and expand upon the knowledge gained through research.

Teaching


Lab Teaching
  • CSE 1010 Introduction to Computing for Engineers (Spring 2020, Fall 2021)
    This course serves as an introduction to the fundamental concepts of computing logic, algorithmic thinking, computing processes, programming languages, and computing environments. By the end of this course, students will have a strong understanding of how to use the computer as a tool to solve various computational problems. The course will cover a range of topics including the principles of computing logic, the process of designing and implementing algorithms, and the use of various programming languages and computing environments. The course will also feature examples and exercises that demonstrate how these concepts can be applied to solve problems in fields such as science, mathematics, and engineering. The goal of this course is to provide students with a solid foundation in the fundamental principles of computing, enabling them to apply their knowledge to real-world problems and further their studies in related areas.
  • CSE 1729 Introduction to Principles of Programming (Spring 2021)
    This course is an introduction to computer programming using a structured programming language. The course will cover the fundamental elements of program design and analysis, and students will learn how to use data and functional abstraction to create correct, efficient, and easy-to-understand programs for a variety of common computing problems. The course will focus on developing the students' understanding of the basic concepts and constructs of programming, such as data types, control structures, functions, and data structures. The students will learn how to use these concepts to solve real-world problems, and will develop the skills needed to design, implement, and test their own programs. The course will also cover best practices for writing efficient and readable code, and will introduce the students to debugging and testing techniques. Overall, this course will provide students with a solid foundation in computer programming, and will prepare them for further studies in software development and other related fields.
Teaching Assistant
  • CSE 4102 Programming Languages (Fall 2020)
    This course is focused on the study of programming languages, specifically examining their features and programming paradigms. Topics covered in the course include data types, control structures, run-time environments, and semantics. The course will also provide an in-depth look at different programming paradigms such as procedural, functional, logical, and object-oriented programming. Additionally, the course will explore features used for parallel and distributed processing, such as threading and networking. Students will also be exposed to both classic and current programming languages and their corresponding environments. The course is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the programming languages, their features and paradigms, and how they can be applied to solve different problems. By the end of the course, students will have a strong understanding of the concepts and techniques needed to create efficient, well-designed and maintainable programs.
  • CSE 3300 Computer Networks and Data Communication (Fall 2019, Spring 2022)
    This course serves as an introduction to the field of computer networks and data communications. The course will cover the fundamental concepts of network types, components, and topology, protocol architecture, routing algorithms, and network performance. The students will learn about different types of networks such as LANs, WANs, and the Internet, and will also learn about the various components that make up these networks, such as hubs, switches, routers, and modems. The course will also cover different types of network protocols, such as TCP/IP, and the principles of routing and flow control. Additionally, the course will explore the performance characteristics of networks and how they can be optimized. Case studies and examples will be used to illustrate the concepts and technologies covered in the course. This course will provide students with a solid understanding of the fundamental concepts of computer networks and data communications, and will prepare them for further studies in related fields such as network engineering, security, and data communications.
  • CSE 2102 Introduction to Software Engineering (Spring 2021)
    This course covers the fundamental concepts of software engineering including the software life cycle and various software development process models. It will introduce students to the different techniques used in software specification, design methodologies, performance analysis, and verification. The course will also cover the importance of team-oriented software design and development, and will provide an introduction to project management techniques. The course will also give students an opportunity to use appropriate design and debugging tools for a modern programming language. Homework and laboratory projects will be used to reinforce the concepts covered in the course and to give students hands-on experience with design and the use/features of a modern programming language. The goal of this course is to provide students with a strong foundation in software engineering concepts and to prepare them for careers in software development, software engineering or related fields.
  • CPS 542 Database Management Systems, CPS 562 Advance Database Management Systems (Fall 2018, Spring 2019)
    This course serves as an introduction to database systems, with a focus on modeling and programming databases. Students will learn about E/R modeling, the theoretical concepts of the relational model, and will gain practical experience working with a commercial DBMS. The course will also cover the integration of databases with the web. Additionally, the course will provide an overview of other database topics such as object-oriented and object-relational databases, multimedia databases, data warehousing, and data mining. However, it is important to note that the focus of the course is not on the implementation of database systems but rather on their modeling, programming and querying. The queries will be presented in relational algebra, SQL, and Datalog and the programming will be done in SQL using Oracle in the UNIX programming environment. The course is designed for students with no prior knowledge of database systems and it will provide them with a solid understanding of the fundamental concepts and principles of databases.

Software Artical
  • Brain nucleus microscope image analysis user interface tool
    The software is designed to perform multiple functions in relation to 3D object of nucleus microscope images. It can display the images, add annotations to them, perform analysis on the images, register them and reconstruct 3D objects from the images. This software is a powerful tool for researchers and scientists who work with microscope images of nuclei, as it allows them to easily view, analyze, and manipulate the images in a variety of ways. The ability to register and reconstruct 3D objects from the images can also provide valuable insights and information that can aid in scientific research and discovery.
  • Prediction of cancer probability using prior and current year images: A user interface
    The software is developed to assess the capability of a novel deep learning model in predicting clinical trials and compare its performance with radiologists. It will provide a user interface to evaluate the prediction ability of the deep learning model and compare it with the results of radiologists. This software could be useful for researchers and medical practitioners who are working in the field of medical imaging and want to evaluate the accuracy and reliability of deep learning models in predicting clinical outcomes.
